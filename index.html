Building a guitar from scratch

This is a project I'm hoping to undertake next semester.

Instructions

  1. Cutting the body
    • Make sure you plan your design well. Measure twice, cut once.
    • Route cavities for pickups, knobs, and quarter-inch jack.
    • Design for comfort - consider adding a cutaway or arm contour.
  2. Cutting the neck
    • There are a few elements to consider here: the neck itself, the truss rod, the headstock, and the fretboard.
    • You want to prepare each element individually, and assemble at the end.
    • For the headstock, you can use excess wood from the neck blank to slant the headstock downward.
    • Drill a cavity through the neck, and insert the truss rod through this cavity.
    • Attach the fretboard using wood glue, then slot fret positions and install the frets.
    • Attach the finished neck to the body using screws or wood glue.
  3. Installing hardware
    • Install pickups, tuners, nut, and bridge into the cavities you drilled earlier.
  4. Wiring the electronics
    • Install the electrical components in their respective cavities.
    • Route wires through the cavities you drilled earlier, and solder them to their corresponding components.
  5. Set up and play!
    • Congratulations! You're now ready to string your guitar and shred to your heart's content. Make sure you tune your strings, and adjust the truss rod and action as necessary.
